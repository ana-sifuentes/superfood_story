The history behind superfoods...


Superfoods are considered better choices than other other non super foods. This marketing term can be misleading and the true origin of these foods is often overlooked or forgotten.

Origin of chia

Chia is actually from mesoamerica, present day Mexico and Guatemala.
It was used by various indigenous groups, like the Aztecs - famous for their strong military, amazing architecture and progressive education.

Lost for almost 500 years

Because of the key role chia had in Aztec society and religion, it was banned during colonization and lost for almost 500 years. Thankfully, chia managed to survive in small, isolated communities.

Rediscovery

Chia resurfaced around the 1930s thanks to a strange combination of terracota decorations, nutritional research and the media.

Growing chia

Chia grows well in arid regions with low quality soils and requires little irrigation once established. You can also grow it on paper towels at home.

What now?

Food is something so essential that we often overlook how complex it is.
Superfoods used to have a role in indigenous diets, colonization and globalization have obscured this fact. While some of these crops are more nutritionally dense end even more sustainable, marketers have stretched their benefits to increase sales. Consumers are vulnerable to misinformation but their purchases end up shaping agriculture practices around the world.
One small seed can have a giant impact.
