Origin of chia

Chia is actually from mesoamerica, present day Mexico and Guatemala.
It was used by various indigenous groups, like the Aztecs - famous for their strong military, amazing architecture and progressive education.

Lost and Rediscovery

Because of the key role chia had in Aztec society and religion, it was banned during colonization and lost for almost 400 years.

It managed to survive in small, isolated communities and it returned thanks to a strange conbination of terracota decorations, nutritional research and the media discussing it.

The myths of chia

Chia seeds are an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, rich in antioxidants, and they provide fiber, iron, and calcium.
Like other plant sources of fiber, they have been linked to good cardiovascular health.
